# CATSS / CCAT User Declaration

For use of the CATSS (Computer Assisted Tools for Septuagint Studies)
morphologically-analyzed Septuagint data — the source layer behind the open
LXX datasets this project may ingest (`eliranwong/LXX-Rahlfs-1935`,
`CenterBLC/LXX`, `codykingham/catss_lxx`).

**How to use this file:** print or export, fill in the fields at the bottom,
sign and date, and keep the signed copy in this repository (this folder). The
historical submission channel (Robert Kraft / CCAT, University of
Pennsylvania) is effectively dormant — the project page was last updated 2004
and the named contact is deceased — so this signed good-faith record, plus the
attribution in [`../spine/ATTRIBUTION.md`](../spine/ATTRIBUTION.md), is how we
honor the terms. If a formal counter-signature is ever wanted, the living
steward is the **IOSCS** (International Organization for Septuagint and Cognate
Studies).

---

## Declaration

In consideration of being granted access to the machine-readable materials
prepared and distributed by the Center for Computer Analysis of Texts (CCAT)
and the CATSS project at the University of Pennsylvania, I agree:

1. **Non-commercial use.** Not to use or make available these materials for
   commercial purposes without first obtaining the written consent of the
   owners/encoders.

2. **Special restrictions.** To observe any special restrictions that may
   govern the use of particular texts or bodies of material.

3. **Control access and propagate terms.** To control access to these
   materials and to require any other party to whom I supply any portion of
   this material to observe these same conditions.

4. **Attribution.** When making formal public reference to the materials, to
   acknowledge appropriately the holder of the copyright and to credit the
   encoder and source (CATSS / CCAT, University of Pennsylvania, and the
   underlying edition).

5. **Report errors.** To report promptly to CCAT any errors discovered in
   these machine-readable materials.

This project is and remains **non-commercial** (its data layer already sits
under non-commercial licenses — BHSA CC BY-NC-SA, OpenHebrewBible CC BY-NC),
so condition (1) is satisfied by design.
